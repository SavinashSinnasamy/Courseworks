Image Gallery

Colombo

The executive and judicial capital and the largest city of Sri Lanka by population. It is often referred to as the capital since Sri Jayawardanepura Kotte, the actual capital, is itself within the urban area of Colombo.

Badulla

The largest city of Uva Provice; situated in the lower central hills of Sri Lanka. The regions varied geography has attracted eco-tourists from all corners of the globe.

Sigiriya

An ancient rock fortress located in the northern district of Matale near the town of Dambulla in the Central Province. Sigiriya today is a UNESCO listed World Heritage Site. It is one of the best preserved examples of ancient urban planning.

Ella

A picturesque hamlet in the Badulla District of Uva Province. The area is known for its rich bio-diversity, dense with numerous varieties of flora and fauna. The flanking hills and its elevation gives it a cool climate.



Kandy

The last capital of the anciet kings' era of Sri Lanka. The city is situated in the midst of hills in the Kandy plateau, which crosses an area if tropical plantantions, mainly tea.

Mirissa

A small town on the south coast of Sri Lanka, located in the Matara District of the Southern Province. Owing to it's beach, vibrant nightlife, and ample Whale and Dolphin wathcing locations; it is a popular tourist destination.

Adams Peak

A sacred mountain in central Sri Lanka.With ties to every major religion in the country, local myths and legends, and geological significance. It has piqued the interest of everyone from far and wide with its ever evolving mythos.

Matale

Located at the heart of the Central Highlands, the region lies in a broad, green fertile valley. Speckled with waterfalls, lakes, grasslands, and mountain ranges, Matale is a yet another booming spot for eco-tourism.



Horton Plains

Encompassing an area of 31.6 km2 and ascending over 2000 m from sea level, it encompasses a region of montane grassland and cloud forest. The plateau is home to innumerabale endemic flora and fauna, rolling hills, major tributaries, impressive waterfalls, and challenging treks.

Anuradhapura

The capital city of the North Central Province. The city's past is inextricably tied to the very history of the country. Being continually occupied for over three mellennia, the ruins that litter the city provides an outline of the Sri Lankan civilisation's growth thorugh the ages.

Sinharaja

A biodiversity hotspot, designated a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. It is the country's last viable area of primary tropical rainforest. Over 60% of the trees, and over 40% of the animals found here are endemic. The forest's dense fog and stygian ambiance has given rise to many folk legends.

Yala

The most visited national park in the country. It boasts a vast array of animals, spread across many ecosystems. Tourists often flock to catch glimpses of the Elephants, Crocodiles and Leopards. Yala stands as a hopeful reminder of the need for environmental conveservation.
