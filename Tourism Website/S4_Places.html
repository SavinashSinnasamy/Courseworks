Top 10 Places to Visit in Sri Lanka


Colombo

The beating heart of the nation, the city is a captivating melange of varying cultures, ethnicities, and denominations. Its history stretches back over 2000 years. Its ports have seen the likes of the ancient Greeks, Persians, Romans, Arabs, Indians and Chinese. Though rife with heritage and history, Colombo's appeal stems from it's night life. A city that lays dormant in the day comes alive at night; the coast which once welcomed ancient foreigners from far and wide for trade now sees itself welcoming them to relish in it's nativity. The city also serves as the executive and judicial capital of the country. It is by and large the most populous city, and often referred to as the de facto capital, since the actual capital city of Sri Jayawardanepura Kotte lies within the urban area of Colombo.

Sigiriya

Arising from forestry, this ancient rock fortress, located in the northern district of Matale near the town of Dambulla in the Central Province, was once thought to be a fable. Until its rediscovery by Major Jonathan Forbes in the early 19th century. Following extensive archealogocal study the fortress was attributed to King Kashyapa I's capital from historical records. The fortress exhibts frescoes, inscriptions, infrastruture that range from the 14th century all the way back to Mesolithic Period. The enigeering of such a megalithic stuructures has long fascinated everyone, with meticulously crafted ladscaped gardens, elaborate urban planning, and sophisticated surface-subsurface hydraulic systems.The fortress now stands as a UNESCO listed World Heritage Site, and as an attestation of the ancient world's engineering prowess.

Ella

Compared to the other places on this list Ella does not boast the historicity nor the mainstream valuation; it is a small picturesque hamlet in the Badulla District of Uva Province. What it does have to boast about is its rich bio-diversity, dense with numerous varieties of flora and fauna. Cloud forests, tea plantations and mountains ranges blanket Ella. The flanking hills and its elavation gives it a cooler climate. With captivating sceneries all around, Ella is a jewel box for toursits seeking escapism.

Kandy

It was the last stronghold of Sri Lanka's final royal dynasty. Records of the city stretch back over a thousand years, when it went by the name Senkadagalapura. The origin of modern Kandy harkens back to when King Senasammata Wickramabahu established the Kingdom of Kandy, who ruled it as a semi-independant kingdom under the Kingdom of Kotte. The kingdom would stand until 1815, when it fell under the British Empire. The allure of Kandy lies not only its heritage but it's geography as well. The city is located between multiple mountain ranges inluding the Knuckles mountain range and the Hanthana mountain range. The plateau also crosses areas of tropical plantantions, mainly tea. It is an ideal spot for those that seek daring and trecherous mountain treks.

Mirissa

A modest fishing town on the south coast of Sri Lanka, located in the Matara Distrcit of the Southern Province. Do not be fooled by its modest description. The town with aquamarine waters is an ideal hotspot for thalassic enthusiats, especially for those that love Whales and Dolphins. Mirissa is rife with spots that showcase the spectacular leviathan up close and personal like nowhere else on Earth. Akin to Colombo; Mirissa has a long standing relatonship with putting up a vibrant show at night: it is a tiny town like no other.

Horton Plains

Originally called "මහ එළිය තැන්න", meaning "Great Open Plain"; true to its primaeval title the area ascends over 2000 m from sea level and sprawls across regions of montane grasslands, cloud forests, swamps, headwaters of the nation's largest rivers, rolling hills, and breathtaking waterfalls. The rugged landscape's charm lies in its unforgiving nature. With massively fluctuating temperatures, humidity, rainfall, sunlight, and sometime gale force winds; the area is perfect for those that seek out a challenging trek through an eco-system that's barely changed in millennia.

Sinharaja

A forest reserve of international significance, one that has been designated a Biosphere Reserve and a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. It is amongst the oldest rainforest on Earth, with the most conservative of estimates putting its formation during the Jurassic period (200 million years to 145 million years ago), and has made a lasting impression on the nation. The forest's dense fog and stygian ambiance has gven rise to many local myths and legends, and been an integral part of the nation's folklore. The very same factors that gave it a mythical quality also hides the nation's greatest treasures: its flora and fauna. Over 60% of the flora, and over 40% of the fauna found in Sinharaja are found no where else on Earth. It is the place of visit for those that seek to peer through time to when the world had no humans to speak of.

Yala

Amongst the most visited national parks in the country. Yala is a vast sanctuary that rnge from monsoon forests to freshwater and marine wetlands. The park is best known for its variety of wildlife ranging from Elephants, Leopards, Crocodiles and birds. Yala is a hopeful reminder of the need For environmental conservation. For those that hope to catch glimpses of the tuskers, muggers and golden panthers, Yala is the perfect spot. The park would serve esepcially well for birdwatchers; with Yala listed as one of the 70 Important Bird Areas (IBAs) in Sri Lanka.

Adams Peak

A sacred mountain in the southern reaches of the the Central Highlands in the Ratnapura District and Nuwara Eliya District of the Sabaragamuwa Province and Central Province. With ties to every majot religion in the country, local myths and legends; the mountain has long piqued the interest of explorers. Adding to its mysticism, the mountain is also rife with gemstones, with yields ranging from Emeralds, Rubies, and Sapphires. It is also the the place where three major rivers of Sri Lanka, Kelani river, Walawe River and the Kalu ganga, are pourpoted to be originating from. Still commanding droves of pilgrims the mythos of Adams Peak is ever evolving. For those that seek a more fantastical place to explore, there is no better place than Adam's Peak.

Anuradhapura

The capital city of the North Central Province. The city's past is inextricably tied to the very fabric of the country. It has seen the rise and fall of numerous kings, invasions, and served as the centre of Theravada Buddhism. Owing to it being inhabited for over three millennia, anicent ruins and infastructure still litter the city, providing an outline of the Sri Lankan civilisation through the ages. It's well-preserved ruins has made it a UNESCO World Heritage Site. For history buffs seeking to be in awe of civilisations of yore: their way of life, their engineering endeavours and their artistic tapestries; there can be no greater substitute to satiate their appetite than Anuradhapura.
